Student Cost of Living Dashboard

As a student living in East Lansing, I became curious about how student living expenses have changed over time and how those changes may continue in the future. While inflation and rising costs are often discussed broadly, I wanted to understand how these trends specifically affect students and their everyday spending patterns. This led me to compare student living costs between 2024 and 2025 and build a simple projection for 2026 to better understand possible future affordability trends.
This project analyzes essential student expense categories including rent, groceries, eating out, transport, and small daily expenses such as coffee. The goal was to create a realistic and relatable cost-of-living analysis using local student-focused pricing rather than relying entirely on generalized national averages.
Using Microsoft Excel for data collection and preprocessing and Power BI for visualization, I built an interactive dashboard that combines data analysis, financial analysis, and business insights into a single platform. The dashboard highlights category-wise expense trends, affordability changes, projected inflation impacts, and how increasing costs may influence student spending behavior over time.

Key Insights
Rent remains the largest contributor to student expenses.
Overall monthly living costs increased between 2024 and 2025.
Projected 2026 trends suggest continued affordability pressure.
Limited student savings may lead to increasingly price-sensitive spending behavior.
